Understanding EBM

What is Evidence Based Medicine?

Evidence Based Medicine (EBM) is the conscientious, explicit, and judicious use of current best evidence in making decisions about the care of individual patients.

Evidence Based Medicine integrates clinical expertise with the best available external clinical evidence from systematic research. It requires the clinician to consider patient values and preferences while applying scientific evidence to clinical decision-making.

The Three Core Pillars of EBM

🔬

Best Research Evidence

Clinically relevant research from basic and clinical sciences, particularly patient-centered research

👨‍⚕️

Clinical Expertise

The proficiency and judgment that individual clinicians acquire through clinical experience and practice

❤️

Patient Values & Preferences

The unique preferences, concerns, and expectations each patient brings to a clinical encounter

Why Report Your Cases?

Reporting clinical cases and research findings according to established guidelines ensures transparency, reproducibility, and impact. Each study type has specific reporting standards — from CARE for case reports to PRISMA for systematic reviews — that help maintain scientific integrity and enable critical appraisal by peers.

Hierarchy of Evidence

The Evidence Pyramid

The pyramid illustrates the hierarchy of evidence, with higher levels representing study designs less susceptible to bias and providing stronger evidence for clinical decisions.

Expert Opinion / Background Level 6
Case Reports / Case Series Level 5
Case-Control Studies Level 4
Cohort Studies Level 3
Randomized Controlled Trials Level 2
Systematic Reviews / Meta-Analyses Level 1
